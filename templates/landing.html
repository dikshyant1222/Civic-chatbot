About Civic-Buddy

Civic-Buddy was founded in 2023 with a mission to bridge the gap between citizens and local government. We believe that technology can empower communities to solve problems collaboratively.

Our platform is designed to make civic engagement simple, transparent, and effective. Whether you're reporting a pothole or participating in a community decision, Civic-Buddy makes it easy.

We partner with local governments and community organizations to ensure your concerns are heard and addressed promptly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about using Civic-Buddy and reporting civic issues.

How do I report a civic issue?

To report an issue, click on the "Report an Issue" button on our homepage. You'll be guided through a simple form where you can describe the problem, add photos if available, and pinpoint the location on a map. Our system will then route your report to the appropriate authorities and keep you updated on progress.

How long does it take to resolve reported issues?

Resolution times vary depending on the type and complexity of the issue. Simple problems like potholes or broken streetlights are typically addressed within 1-2 weeks, while more complex issues may take longer. You can track progress in real-time through your account dashboard, which shows updates from the responsible department.

Is there a cost to use Civic-Buddy?

Civic-Buddy is completely free for citizens to use. Our platform is supported through partnerships with local governments and community organizations who are committed to improving civic engagement. For legal services, lawyers may charge fees which will be clearly communicated upfront.

How can I get involved in community decisions?

Our Community Engagement section allows you to participate in polls, vote on local initiatives, and join discussion forums. You can also attend virtual town halls and public meetings listed on our event calendar. Sign up for notifications to stay informed about opportunities to contribute to decisions affecting your neighborhood.

What types of issues can I report?

You can report a wide range of civic issues including road and sidewalk problems, public sanitation issues, malfunctioning street lights, public safety concerns, park maintenance, noise complaints, and more. We also provide templates for common legal issues. If you're unsure whether your issue qualifies, feel free to report it anyway and we'll direct it appropriately.
